It suffices to find a relation between '<' and '⊆': $\{x \le a\} \subseteq \{x \le b\} \iff a \le b$.

Consider any number $t$ between $m$ and $M$ inclusive. Normalize it with: $\alpha = \frac{(t-m)}{M-m}$. So, every $t$ is mapped one-to-one to $\alpha$. In particular, take $m = 0$ and $M=2$ which contains $[0, 1]$ !
